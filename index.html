About Annai Amala Trust

It was founded in the year 1995 by S. Jesudass Raja on land donated to Amala by his father. Amala Children's Home and St. Agnes' School began with just two huts and 30 children. Today, it is a modern complex of facilities boarding 280 orphans and educating 750 students.

Our Vision

Amala Children's Home is a non-profitable organization which seeks to secure and promote every child's potential in an improved area and empower them to build a better future for themselves.

Our Mission

To improve their lifestyles and eradicate poverty by providing essential skills and education. To offer tools for self-sufficiency, administering with dignity, compassion, and respect.

Our Institutions

Amala Children's Home

Provides a safe, stimulating, and loving environment for destitute and orphaned children. Girls and boys are housed separately next to our school. The children are educated by day and enjoy extracurriculars in the evening.

  • Safe Environment
  • Balanced Nutritious Diet
  • Integrated Development

St. Agnes' School

We have dedicated teachers among whom few are here ever since it started. The students not only learn basics like Language, Maths, and Science but also life skills.

  • 23 Classrooms
  • Science & Computer Labs
  • Vocational Training

Amala Old Age Home

We take care of old age people who were abandoned by families and socially neglected. We provide food, clothing, medical aid, and accommodation free of cost for their happy and peaceful life.

Community Outreach

Vocational Training

Empowering women and youth with skills like Tailoring, Computer Basics, and Handicrafts to ensure financial independence.

  • Certified Courses
  • Job Placement Support

Medical Camps

Regular free medical camps for the surrounding villages, providing general checkups, eye care, and basic medicines.

Evening Study Halls

Safe spaces for village children to study after school hours with the help of volunteer tutors.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I sponsor a child?

You can sponsor a child's education, food, or overall care by setting up a monthly or yearly donation. Please contact us for details.

Can I visit the home?

Yes! We welcome visitors. Spending time with our children is a gift in itself. Please call ahead to schedule your visit.

Are my donations tax-exempt?

Yes, all donations to Annai Amala Trust are eligible for tax exemption under section 80G of the Income Tax Act.

Do you accept in-kind donations?

Absolutely. Rice, groceries, clothes, and stationeries are always needed. Please check with our office for current needs.
